# Launch Plan

## 1. Purpose
Defines the minimal structured plan for exposing the MVP to real users within the Venture Operating System (VOS).

This plan ensures controlled, observable, and traceable launch behavior aligned with MVP constraints.

---

## 2. Core Principle
A launch is a **controlled experiment design**, not a distribution strategy.

---

## 3. Launch Composition
A valid Launch Plan includes:

### 3.1 Target User Slice
A minimal, well-defined subset of users used for initial validation.

### 3.2 Entry Point Definition
The exact point where users enter MVP flows.

### 3.3 Exposure Constraints
Rules governing what parts of the MVP are visible and accessible.

### 3.4 Observation Strategy
Mechanism for capturing behavioral signals during interaction.

### 3.5 Feedback Capture Mechanism
Structured collection of validation data from users.

---

## 4. Launch Constraints
Launch Plan MUST:

- remain strictly within MVP scope
- expose only validated flows
- preserve execution traceability

Launch Plan MUST NOT:

- introduce new features
- expand product scope
- modify execution or architecture layers

---

## 5. Success Criteria
A Launch Plan is valid if it:

- enables at least one full MVP flow exposure
- produces measurable feedback signals
- preserves traceability to execution units
