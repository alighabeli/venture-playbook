# Launch Framework

## 1. Purpose
Defines how a validated MVP is transitioned into real-world exposure within the Venture Operating System (VOS), enabling structured feedback collection, behavioral observation, and initial market interaction.

This layer operationalizes the first external release of the system.

It does NOT define marketing strategy, growth tactics, or implementation details.

---

## 2. Core Principle
Launch is not promotion.
Launch is **controlled exposure of a validated system to reality**.

---

## 3. Launch Objectives
A Launch must:

- expose MVP flows to real users
- generate measurable behavioral data
- validate or invalidate MVP assumptions
- preserve traceability to execution and architecture layers

---

## 4. Launch Scope
Includes:
- controlled user access to MVP flows
- observation of system behavior in real conditions
- structured feedback collection

Excludes:
- full-scale marketing campaigns
- growth optimization strategies
- product expansion beyond MVP scope

---

## 5. Launch Structure
A Launch consists of:

### 5.1 Exposure Layer
Controlled availability of MVP flows to external users

### 5.2 Observation Layer
Monitoring of user interaction with system flows

### 5.3 Feedback Layer
Collection of structured signals for validation

---

## 6. Launch Constraints
Launch MUST:
- remain within MVP scope boundaries
- preserve execution traceability
- avoid introducing new system behavior

Launch MUST NOT:
- alter architecture
- modify execution model
- expand product scope

---

## 7. Dependency Rules
Launch depends on:
- 08-MVP layer
- 07-Execution layer
- 06-Architecture layer

---

## 8. Role in VOS
This layer is the first controlled interaction between the system and real-world users, enabling empirical validation of prior system assumptions.
