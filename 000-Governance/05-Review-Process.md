# Review Process — Venture Playbook OS

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the review process used to evaluate documents, stage outputs, templates, and system changes.

The objective is to ensure:

- Quality
- Consistency
- Traceability
- Structural compliance
- Decision readiness

Review is mandatory before approval.

---

## 2. Review Principles

Review exists to:

- Detect errors
- Identify ambiguity
- Challenge weak reasoning
- Verify compliance
- Improve decision quality

Review is not a formality.

---

## 3. Review Scope

The following items require review:

- Governance documents
- Stage outputs
- Templates
- ADRs
- AI contracts
- Major structural changes

---

## 4. Review Types

### Structural Review

Evaluates:

- compliance with system architecture
- stage boundaries
- document standards
- governance rules

---

### Quality Review

Evaluates:

- clarity
- completeness
- consistency
- usability

---

### Decision Review

Evaluates:

- assumptions
- evidence
- alternatives
- decision rationale

---

### AI Review

Evaluates:

- AI contract compliance
- output structure
- missing inputs
- assumption handling

---

## 5. Review Workflow

All reviews follow:

```text
Submission
→ Review
→ Findings
→ Revisions
→ Approval
```

---

## 6. Review Outcomes

### Approved

Ready for use.

No material issues identified.

---

### Approved with Notes

Usable but improvements recommended.

---

### Revision Required

Issues must be resolved before approval.

---

### Rejected

Fundamental problems identified.

Requires redesign or resubmission.

---

## 7. Review Checklist

Every review must evaluate:

### Clarity

Is the content understandable?

---

### Consistency

Does it align with system rules?

---

### Traceability

Can outputs be traced to inputs?

---

### Assumption Management

Are assumptions explicitly labeled?

---

### Evidence Quality

Is evidence sufficient and credible?

---

### Structural Compliance

Does it follow required standards?

---

### Decision Readiness

Can informed decisions be made?

---

## 8. AI Reviewer Responsibilities

AI reviewers should:

- identify missing information
- detect contradictions
- flag assumptions presented as facts
- verify structural compliance
- suggest improvements

AI reviewers do not approve outputs.

---

## 9. Human Reviewer Responsibilities

Human reviewers should:

- exercise judgment
- assess business relevance
- evaluate strategic implications
- approve or reject outcomes

Final approval belongs to humans.

---

## 10. Approval Rule

Approval requires:

- review completion
- no unresolved critical issues
- compliance with governance rules

---

## 11. Escalation Rule

Escalate review when:

- uncertainty remains high
- architectural impact exists
- governance impact exists
- irreversible decisions are involved

Escalation may require an ADR.

---

## 12. Final Principle

Review is a decision quality mechanism.

The objective of review is not agreement.

The objective of review is confidence.
