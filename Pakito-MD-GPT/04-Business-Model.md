# Business Model

## Business Model Type

Pakito is a two-sided service marketplace.

The platform creates value by connecting demand and supply in the laundry and cleaning services market and captures a portion of the transaction value generated between them.

---

# Economic Actors

## Demand Side

### Customers

Individuals who purchase laundry and cleaning services.

Role:
- Create demand
- Pay for services
- Generate transaction volume

---

## Supply Side

### Laundry Partners

Independent service providers fulfilling customer orders.

Role:
- Deliver services
- Generate supply capacity
- Receive payment for completed work

---

## Platform

### Pakito

Coordinator and market infrastructure layer.

Role:
- Customer acquisition
- Demand aggregation
- Order management
- Trust and quality layer
- Transaction orchestration

---

# Revenue Model

## Primary Revenue Stream

### Transaction Commission

Pakito receives a percentage of each completed order.

Current Assumption:

- Commission Rate: 15%

Revenue Formula:

Platform Revenue =
Order Value × Commission Rate

---

# Economic Engine

The business model succeeds when:

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

is significantly lower than

Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

and when

Gross Profit Per Order

remains positive after operational costs.

# Business Model Constraints

The model depends on:

- Sustainable unit economics
- Reliable service providers
- Repeat customer behavior
- Controlled acquisition costs
- Operational quality

Failure in any of these areas can invalidate growth assumptions.

---

# Open Assumptions

The following assumptions remain unvalidated:

- Average Order Value = 800,000 Toman
- Commission Rate = 15%

- Customer Acquisition Cost
