# Glossary — Venture Playbook OS

## 1. Purpose

This glossary defines the canonical vocabulary used throughout the Venture Playbook OS.

All documents, templates, AI agents, and contributors should use these definitions consistently.

If a term is not defined here, its meaning is not considered authoritative.

---

# A

## Activity

A specific action performed within a stage to produce a deliverable or advance validation.

---

## ADR (Architecture Decision Record)

A formal document used to record significant and irreversible architectural decisions.

---

## AI Agent

An AI system operating within a defined stage context and according to an explicit AI Contract.

AI agents assist execution but do not possess decision authority.

---

## AI Contract

A specification defining:

- responsibilities
- constraints
- inputs
- outputs
- behaviors

for an AI operating within a stage.

---

# C

## Capability

A reusable function that may be used across multiple stages.

Examples:

- Analytics
- Sales
- Marketing
- Customer Success

Capabilities are not lifecycle stages.

---

## Complexity Level

A classification representing the overall uncertainty, risk, and structural complexity of a venture.

Levels:

- Level 1 (Simple)
- Level 2 (Standard)
- Level 3 (Complex)

Complexity Level influences governance rigor and confidence thresholds.

---

## Confidence Score

A normalized measure of readiness ranging from:

```text
0.00 – 1.00
```

used to assess progression readiness and decision confidence.

---

# D

## Decision

A selected course of action based on available evidence, assumptions, and constraints.

---

## Deliverable

A defined output produced by a stage.

Deliverables exist to support decisions and execution.

---

# E

## Evidence

Information used to support, validate, or challenge assumptions and decisions.

Evidence should be attributable and verifiable.

---

## Exit Criteria

Conditions that must be satisfied before a stage can be considered complete.

---

# G

## Governance

The system of rules, controls, reviews, and decision authority that preserves system integrity.

---

# H

## Human Authority

The principle that final approval and decision-making responsibility belong to humans.

---

# I

## Input

Information, artifacts, assumptions, or decisions required to begin work within a stage.

---

# M

## Meta-Model

The conceptual structure defining how core entities relate to each other.

Examples:

- Decisions
- Assumptions
- Evidence
- Experiments
- Deliverables

---

# O

## Objective

The intended outcome a stage or activity seeks to achieve.

---

## Output

Any artifact, deliverable, decision, or result produced by a stage.

---

# P

## Playbook

The complete operating system governing venture creation and validation.

---

## Principle

A foundational rule used to guide system design and execution.

---

# R

## Review

A structured evaluation intended to improve confidence and decision quality.

---

# S

## Stage

A bounded execution context within the venture lifecycle.

Each stage has:

- Inputs
- Activities
- Deliverables
- Exit Criteria

---

## Stage Boundary

The explicit limits defining what work belongs inside a stage.

---

## Stage Transition

The controlled movement from one stage to another after exit criteria are satisfied.

---

# T

## Template

A reusable structure used to standardize execution artifacts.

---

## Traceability

The ability to connect outputs back to inputs, assumptions, evidence, and decisions.

---

# U

## Uncertainty

Information that is incomplete, unknown, or insufficiently validated.

Uncertainty must be explicitly acknowledged.

---

# V

## Validation

The process of reducing uncertainty through evidence and experimentation.

---

## Venture

A coordinated effort to transform an idea into a validated product and business.

---

## Venture Playbook OS

A constraint-driven operating system for venture creation, validation, and growth.

---

## Version

A controlled identifier representing the state of a system, stage, template, or artifact.

---

# Final Principle

Shared vocabulary is a prerequisite for shared understanding.

A system without controlled terminology cannot achieve consistent execution.
