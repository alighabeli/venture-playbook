# Customer Discovery Framework

## 1. Purpose
Defines how structured learning signals are translated into validated understanding of real customers within the Venture Operating System (VOS).

This layer focuses on identifying who the customer is, how they behave, and what validated problems they actually experience based on observed evidence.

---

## 2. Core Principle
Customer understanding is not assumed.
It is **derived from structured behavioral evidence**, not interviews or opinions alone.

---

## 3. Inputs
This layer consumes:

- Learning signals
- MVP flow behavior data
- Launch observation outputs
- Execution traceability logs

---

## 4. Outputs

### 4.1 Validated Customer Profiles
Evidence-based customer definitions grounded in behavior

### 4.2 Behavioral Segments
Clusters of users grouped by observable patterns

### 4.3 Problem Validation Results
Confirmation or rejection of problem assumptions per segment

---

## 5. Customer Definition Rule
A customer is valid only if:

- their behavior is observable through MVP flows
- their actions map to execution units
- their needs are inferred from repeated signals, not isolated data

---

## 6. Segmentation Principle
Segmentation is based on:

- behavioral patterns
- flow completion rates
- interaction consistency

Not based on demographic assumptions alone.

---

## 7. Validation Constraints
Customer insights MUST NOT:

- introduce new unvalidated assumptions
- rely purely on stated preferences
- contradict learning signals

---

## 8. Role in VOS
This layer anchors the system in real user behavior and ensures that product and execution decisions are grounded in validated customer reality.
