# Product-Market Fit (PMF) Framework

## 1. Purpose
Defines how the Venture Operating System (VOS) determines whether a validated product actually fits a real market need based on accumulated evidence from Learning and Customer Discovery layers.

This layer formalizes the transition from validated problem understanding to confirmed market fit or non-fit.

---

## 2. Core Principle
PMF is not declared.
It is **inferred from consistent behavioral evidence across multiple validated customers and flows**.

---

## 3. Inputs
This layer consumes:

- Customer Discovery outputs
- Learning signals and signal clusters
- MVP flow performance data
- Launch behavioral observations

---

## 4. Outputs

### 4.1 PMF Status
One of:
- PMF NOT ACHIEVED
- PMF PARTIAL
- PMF CONFIRMED

### 4.2 Fit Evidence Map
Mapping of which flows and segments show alignment or misalignment

### 4.3 Gap Analysis
Structural or behavioral gaps preventing PMF

---

## 5. PMF Evaluation Rule
PMF is valid only if:

- multiple independent customer segments show consistent positive engagement
- core MVP flows are repeatedly completed without structural friction
- learning signals converge toward stable patterns

---

## 6. Anti-Pattern Rules
PMF must NOT be declared based on:

- isolated success cases
- anecdotal user feedback
- non-representative samples

---

## 7. Transition Conditions

### Enter PMF Stage:
- MVP launched
- Learning signals stabilized
- Customer segments validated

### Exit PMF Stage:
- PMF confirmed OR rejected with clear structural reasons

---

## 8. Role in VOS
This layer acts as the final validation checkpoint before scaling decisions and growth system design.
