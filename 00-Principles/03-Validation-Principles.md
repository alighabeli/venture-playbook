# Validation Principles — Venture Playbook OS

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how validation is performed within the Venture Playbook OS.

Validation is the system’s primary mechanism for reducing uncertainty and improving decision quality.

---

## 2. Principle of Uncertainty Reduction

The goal of validation is not to prove correctness.

The goal is to reduce uncertainty to an acceptable level for decision-making.

---

## 3. Principle of Testable Assumptions

Only assumptions that are:

- explicitly stated
- structurally isolated
- testable

can be validated.

Untestable assumptions must be flagged as high risk.

---

## 4. Principle of Hypothesis Formation

Every validation activity must begin with a hypothesis:

- what is being tested
- expected outcome
- failure condition

Without a hypothesis, validation is invalid.

---

## 5. Principle of Evidence Generation

Validation must produce:

- observable evidence
- measurable signals (when possible)
- documented outcomes

Opinion is not evidence.

---

## 6. Principle of Progressive Validation

Validation occurs in layers:

- Concept validation
- Problem validation
- Solution validation
- Market validation
- Product validation

Each layer reduces a specific type of uncertainty.

---

## 7. Principle of Minimum Validation Effort

Validation effort must be proportional to:

- complexity level
- decision criticality
- reversibility of outcome

Over-validation is waste.

Under-validation is risk.

---

## 8. Principle of Early Failure Detection

Validation must prioritize:

- identifying failure early
- rejecting invalid assumptions quickly
- minimizing sunk cost

---

## 9. Principle of Signal over Opinion

Only signals derived from:

- user behavior
- experiments
- data
- structured interviews

are valid.

Opinions are secondary inputs, not validation outputs.

---

## 10. Principle of Controlled Experimentation

All validation experiments must define:

- variables
- success criteria
- measurement method
- expected outcome

---

## 11. Principle of AI-Assisted Validation Design

AI may:

- design experiments
- suggest hypotheses
- structure validation frameworks
- analyze results

AI may NOT:

- declare validation success without evidence
- bypass experimental design requirements

---

## 12. Principle of Iterative Refinement

Validation is iterative:

- results refine assumptions
- assumptions refine experiments
- experiments refine decisions

No validation is final.

---

## 13. Principle of Validation Boundaries

Validation must remain within stage scope.

Cross-stage validation contamination is prohibited.

---

## 14. Principle of Evidence Hierarchy

Evidence is ranked:

1. Behavioral data (strongest)
2. Experiment results
3. Structured interviews
4. Observations
5. Opinions (weakest)

---

## 15. Final Principle

Validation is not about confirmation.

Validation is about disciplined reduction of uncertainty until decisions become safe enough to execute.
