# Complexity Principles — Venture Playbook OS

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how complexity is classified, interpreted, and used to adjust governance rigor within the Venture Playbook OS.

Complexity is not descriptive only — it is operational.

It directly influences:

- validation depth
- decision rigor
- documentation strictness
- AI behavior constraints

---

## 2. Principle of Explicit Complexity Classification

Every venture, stage, or initiative must be assigned a complexity level:

- Level 1 — Simple
- Level 2 — Moderate
- Level 3 — Complex

Unclassified complexity is invalid.

---

## 3. Principle of Complexity-Driven Governance

Governance strictness scales with complexity:

### Level 1

- Lightweight validation
- Minimal documentation
- Fast iteration cycles

### Level 2

- Standard validation depth
- Structured documentation required
- Moderate review rigor

### Level 3

- Full validation discipline
- Strict governance enforcement
- Mandatory ADR usage for major decisions

---

## 4. Principle of Uncertainty-Complexity Coupling

Complexity is directly proportional to:

- uncertainty level
- number of dependencies
- reversibility cost
- system coupling

Higher uncertainty increases effective complexity.

---

## 5. Principle of Adaptive Rigor

System rigor must adapt dynamically:

- Higher complexity → stricter validation
- Lower complexity → faster execution

Over-rigidity in low complexity is waste.
Under-rigidity in high complexity is failure.

---

## 6. Principle of Decision Threshold Scaling

Decision thresholds are adjusted by complexity level:

### Level 1

- lower evidence requirement
- faster decisions allowed

### Level 2

- balanced evidence requirement
- structured trade-off analysis

### Level 3

- high evidence requirement
- formal validation required before decision

---

## 7. Principle of AI Constraint Scaling

AI behavior constraints scale with complexity:

### Level 1

- more autonomy in structuring outputs
- lightweight validation support

### Level 2

- structured outputs required
- explicit uncertainty handling

### Level 3

- strict adherence to all principles
- no inference without explicit context
- mandatory clarification requests

---

## 8. Principle of Complexity Transparency

Complexity level must always be:

- explicitly stated
- justified
- reviewable

Hidden complexity classification is prohibited.

---

## 9. Principle of Complexity Drift Prevention

Complexity must not silently increase over time.

Any increase in complexity requires:

- justification
- review
- documentation update

---

## 10. Principle of Minimum Necessary Complexity

Systems should not be more complex than required.

Complexity must be reduced whenever possible without losing correctness.

---

## 11. Principle of Decomposition for Complexity Control

High complexity systems must be decomposed into:

- smaller stages
- isolated decisions
- independent validation units

Decomposition is a primary control mechanism.

---

## 12. Principle of Complexity-Aware Validation

Validation depth must scale with complexity:

- Low complexity → lightweight validation
- High complexity → structured multi-layer validation

---

## 13. Principle of Reversibility Sensitivity

Higher complexity increases cost of reversal.

Therefore:

- irreversible decisions require stricter validation at higher complexity levels

---

## 14. Principle of Complexity Awareness in AI

AI must:

- recognize complexity level
- adjust reasoning depth accordingly
- enforce stricter constraints at higher levels

---

## 15. Final Principle

Complexity is not a property of ideas.

It is a property of systems under uncertainty and constraint interaction.
