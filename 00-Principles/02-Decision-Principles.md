# Decision Principles — Venture Playbook OS

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how decisions must be made within the Venture Playbook OS.

It governs all decision-making across stages, AI interactions, and venture execution.

---

## 2. Principle of Evidence-Weighted Decisions

All decisions must be weighted by:

- evidence strength
- assumption confidence
- uncertainty level

Decisions based purely on intuition are invalid unless explicitly marked.

---

## 3. Principle of Explicit Options

Every non-trivial decision must include:

- at least two alternatives
- rationale for each option
- trade-off comparison

Single-option decisions are considered incomplete reasoning.

---

## 4. Principle of Decision Traceability

Each decision must include:

- input context
- assumptions
- reasoning path
- selected option
- rejected alternatives

Untraceable decisions are invalid.

---

## 5. Principle of Reversibility-Aware Decisioning

All decisions must be classified by reversibility:

- Reversible
- Semi-Reversible
- Irreversible

Higher rigor is required for less reversible decisions.

---

## 6. Principle of Uncertainty Isolation

Uncertainty must never be hidden inside decisions.

It must be explicitly separated into:

- knowns
- unknowns
- assumptions

---

## 7. Principle of Minimum Decision Scope

Decisions must be scoped to the smallest valid unit.

Over-broad decisions:

- reduce traceability
- increase system risk
- reduce adaptability

---

## 8. Principle of Delay When Uncertain

If uncertainty exceeds acceptable thresholds:

- delay decision
- gather more evidence
- run validation step

Default behavior under uncertainty is not speculation.

---

## 9. Principle of Evidence Sufficiency

A decision is valid only when:

- evidence is sufficient for the complexity level
- uncertainty is within acceptable bounds
- assumptions are explicitly labeled

---

## 10. Principle of Option Evaluation Discipline

Each option must be evaluated across:

- feasibility
- risk
- validation cost
- expected impact
- reversibility

---

## 11. Principle of AI-Assisted Decision Structuring

AI may:

- structure decision options
- analyze trade-offs
- surface risks
- propose alternatives

AI may NOT:

- finalize decisions
- bypass missing information
- collapse options prematurely

---

## 12. Principle of Decision Logging

All significant decisions must be recorded as:

- explicit decision record
- rationale
- context
- alternatives rejected

This enables system learning and traceability.

---

## 13. Principle of No Hidden Decisions

No decision may be embedded implicitly in:

- narrative text
- assumptions
- templates
- stage outputs

All decisions must be explicitly declared.

---

## 14. Principle of Context Completeness

Decisions must be made with:

- full available context
- explicit identification of missing context
- awareness of uncertainty impact

---

## 15. Final Principle

A decision is not an opinion.

A decision is a traceable commitment under uncertainty.
