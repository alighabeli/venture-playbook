# Go-To-Market (GTM) Framework

## 1. Purpose
Defines how a validated and PMF-confirmed system is introduced into the market through structured positioning, distribution design, and controlled expansion of access within the Venture Operating System (VOS).

This layer governs market entry logic, not marketing execution.

---

## 2. Core Principle
GTM is not promotion.
GTM is **systematic activation of validated value delivery in defined market channels**.

---

## 3. Inputs
This layer consumes:

- PMF validation outputs
- Growth strategy loops
- Customer behavioral segmentation
- MVP flow performance data

---

## 4. Outputs

### 4.1 Positioning Definition
Clear articulation of value positioning based on validated behavior

### 4.2 Distribution Model
Structured pathways through which users access the system

### 4.3 Channel Strategy
Definition of controlled acquisition channels aligned with validated demand

---

## 5. GTM Constraints
GTM MUST:

- remain consistent with PMF-defined value
- preserve execution traceability
- align with validated customer segments

GTM MUST NOT:

- introduce unvalidated market assumptions
- redefine product value proposition
- bypass learning or execution layers

---

## 6. GTM Structure
GTM is composed of:

### 6.1 Market Entry Point
Defined initial access point for target users

### 6.2 Channel Activation Strategy
How users are systematically brought into MVP or scaled flows

### 6.3 Positioning Logic
How system value is communicated based on validated outcomes

---

## 7. Role in VOS
This layer connects internal system validation with external market presence, enabling controlled and traceable market entry.
