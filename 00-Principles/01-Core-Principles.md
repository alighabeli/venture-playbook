# Core Principles — Venture Playbook OS

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the fundamental principles that govern all venture creation within the Venture Playbook OS.

These principles are universal, invariant, and system-defining.

They apply to all stages, decisions, and AI interactions.

---

## 2. Principle of Evidence-Based Reality

No statement is valid unless it is:

- supported by evidence
- explicitly marked as an assumption
- explicitly marked as uncertainty

Reality in the system is constructed through evidence, not belief.

---

## 3. Principle of Structured Uncertainty

Uncertainty is not eliminated; it is structured.

All uncertainty must be:

- identified
- classified
- measured when possible
- reduced through validation

Unstructured uncertainty is considered system failure.

---

## 4. Principle of Traceable Decisions

Every decision must be traceable to:

- input data
- assumptions
- evidence
- reasoning path

Decisions without traceability are invalid within the system.

---

## 5. Principle of Stage Isolation

Each stage operates as an independent execution context.

Rules:

- No cross-stage execution logic
- No premature assumptions from future stages
- No hidden dependencies

Stage boundaries must remain strict.

---

## 6. Principle of Constraint-Driven Execution

Execution is governed by constraints, not intuition.

Constraints define:

- what is allowed
- what is required
- what is prohibited

Creativity operates within constraints, not outside them.

---

## 7. Principle of Decision Responsibility

Humans retain final authority for all decisions.

AI systems:

- assist reasoning
- structure information
- highlight risks
- propose options

AI does not decide.

---

## 8. Principle of Explicit Assumptions

All assumptions must be explicitly declared.

Implicit assumptions are treated as errors.

Assumptions must be:

- labeled
- isolated
- testable when possible

---

## 9. Principle of Iterative Validation

Validation is continuous, not a single phase.

Every stage must:

- test assumptions
- reduce uncertainty
- refine understanding

No idea is valid without iterative validation.

---

## 10. Principle of Complexity Alignment

System rigor must match venture complexity.

- Simple ventures → lightweight validation
- Complex ventures → strict validation and governance

Over-engineering is a failure mode.

Under-structuring is also a failure mode.

---

## 11. Principle of AI Bounded Execution

AI operates strictly within:

- defined stage context
- provided inputs
- explicit constraints

AI must not:

- infer missing context silently
- skip validation steps
- override governance rules

---

## 12. Principle of Minimum Necessary Structure

The system must remain as simple as possible while preserving correctness.

Structure is added only when:

- required by validation
- required by complexity
- required by governance

Unused structure is system debt.

---

## 13. Principle of Reversibility Awareness

All decisions must consider reversibility:

- reversible
- semi-reversible
- irreversible

Irreversible decisions require higher scrutiny and formal documentation.

---

## 14. Principle of System Integrity

System consistency is more important than local optimization.

No stage or decision may violate:

- governance rules
- traceability requirements
- validation requirements

---

## 15. Final Principle

A venture is not created by ideas.

It is created by disciplined execution under structured uncertainty.
